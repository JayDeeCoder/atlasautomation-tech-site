The Full Pitch Script
Memorize the opener and 3 qualifying questions — improvise the rest
Walk-Up Opener (at the shop/salon/restaurant)
"Hi! Are you the owner? [Wait for yes] Perfect — I'm Jay with Atlas Automation Group. We help local businesses like yours get way more Google reviews and bring back customers who haven't been in a while — all through automated texts. Takes 2 minutes to show you. Do you have a second?"
Keep it short. You're not pitching yet — you're getting permission to pitch. If they say no or they're busy, ask: "What time do you have 5 minutes?" and come back.

3 Qualifying Questions

Ask these in order. They're designed to get the owner to identify their own pain.

Question 1 — Google Reviews
"Quick question — how do most of your new customers find you? [Let them answer] Google, right? How many reviews do you have right now — and when did the last one come in?"
They almost always say "not enough" or "not lately." This opens the door. If they're doing well with reviews, pivot: "That's amazing. Are you doing any follow-up texts to bring back people who haven't come in a while?"
Question 2 — Repeat Customers
"What percentage of your customers come back on their own, versus you having to do something to remind them? [Let them answer] Right — most businesses lose 60–70% of customers to silence. Not bad service. Just no follow-up."
Question 3 — The Setup
"Are you doing any automated follow-up right now — texts, emails, anything? [Usually: no] That's what we do. Let me show you exactly what it would look like for [BUSINESS NAME]."
Pull out the laptop or phone and open live-demo.html. Enter their info and let them watch the messages build in real time. This is your demo.

Daily Routine Checklist
What a productive sales day looks like

Aim for 10 walk-in pitches per day. You'll convert 1–3 of them. That's 5–15 new clients per week with enough hustle.

  • 8:00am — Plan your route. Pick a neighborhood with 20+ target businesses (auto shops, salons, gyms, restaurants). Google Maps works great.
  • 8:30am — Charge your phone, make sure the live demo page is saved locally (works offline for previews).
  • 9:00am — First stop. Auto shops and gyms open early — hit those first.
  • 9am–12pm — Morning block: target auto shops, gyms, barbershops (owners are usually free before it gets busy).
  • 12–1pm — Avoid restaurants during lunch rush. Grab food, check messages from Jay.
  • 1pm–3pm — Hit salons, spas, wellness centers (midday is their slow period).
  • 3pm–5pm — Restaurant window (between lunch and dinner service). Best time to get an owner's attention.
  • 5pm — Text Jay with your day's results: pitches made, demos given, signups closed.
  • 5:30pm — Follow up with any "think about it" owners via text. Keep it brief: "Hey [NAME] — just wanted to make sure you have my number if any questions come up about the trial. — [YOUR NAME], Atlas Automation Group"
  • Evening — Research tomorrow's route. Look up Google review counts for target businesses — low counts = hot prospects.
The best leads are businesses with under 50 Google reviews. Search "auto shop near [city]" and sort by review count. The ones with 10–30 reviews are desperate for what you're selling — they just don't know it exists yet.

Stall
▼
"Of course — can I ask, is it more a budget question or a 'let me make sure we want to do this' question?"

If budget: run the free trial angle — no money involved today. If approval: "Is there any chance they'd have 5 minutes today? I can walk you both through it together. The trial is same-day only so I don't want you to miss out."
